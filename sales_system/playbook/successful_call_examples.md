# Successful Cold Call Examples

Real examples of what good calls sound like. Study these patterns.

---

## Example 1: The Perfect Call (Booked in 4 Minutes)

**Context:** Managing broker, 15 agents, Louisville KY
**AI Opening Hook:** "Being named the first woman to lead HomeServices KOI—overseeing 1,100 agents—that's a massive responsibility."

```
CALLER: "Hi Stacy, this is John with AI Acceleration.
         I saw the news about you being named the first woman
         to lead HomeServices KOI. Congrats—that's huge."

STACY:  "Oh, thank you! How can I help you?"

CALLER: "I help brokerages like yours get agents using AI
         in ways that actually save time. Mind if I ask
         one quick question?"

STACY:  "Sure, go ahead."

CALLER: "What's your biggest bottleneck right now—
         marketing, operations, or agent productivity?"

STACY:  "Honestly? Agent productivity. My top agents are
         crushing it, but the middle tier is struggling
         to keep up with listings and follow-up."

CALLER: "Got it. So the gap between top and middle
         performers is the issue?"

STACY:  "Exactly. And it's hard to scale what the
         top ones do naturally."

CALLER: "That's exactly what we solve. We do a one-day
         workshop where agents build AI systems for
         listings, emails, social—the admin stuff.

         If AI saves each agent 5 hours a week,
         that's $500/month in recovered time.
         For your team, that's real money.

         The workshop is one day, on-site.
         Agents leave using it Monday morning."

STACY:  "Interesting. What does it cost?"

CALLER: "$5,000 for the full day. For 15 agents,
         that's about $330 per agent.
         Pays for itself in the first month."

STACY:  "That's not bad actually. When would you do it?"

CALLER: "I have availability in January. Would a
         Tuesday or Wednesday work better for your team?"

STACY:  "Wednesday is usually lighter. Let me check...
         The 15th could work."

CALLER: "Perfect. Let me send you a calendar invite
         for a 15-minute call first to walk through
         the curriculum. Does 2pm on Thursday work
         to nail down details?"

STACY:  "That works."

CALLER: "Great. I'll send the invite now. Looking
         forward to it, Stacy."

STACY:  "Thanks, John. Talk then."
```

**Why It Worked:**
- Personalized hook (referenced specific achievement)
- Asked permission before pitching
- Let her talk first (discovered the real pain)
- Connected solution to her specific problem
- Gave price confidently when asked
- Closed with specific alternatives
- Scheduled next step immediately

## Example 2: Handling "Too Busy" (Booked in 6 Minutes)

**Context:** Broker/Owner, 8 agents, Nashville TN
**AI Opening Hook:** "20 years building Riverside Realty from scratch—that's impressive staying power in this market."

```
CALLER: "Hi Mark, this is Sarah with AI Acceleration.
         I'm calling because I saw you've been building
         Riverside Realty for 20 years—that's real
         staying power in Nashville."

MARK:   "Thanks. Look, I'm actually pretty busy
         right now. Can you make it quick?"

CALLER: "Totally. One question: Are your agents
         spending hours on listing descriptions
         and follow-up emails?"

MARK:   "Yeah, everybody is. That's just the job."

CALLER: "What if it didn't have to be? We help
         brokerages cut that admin time in half
         with AI. One-day workshop, your office.
         Agents leave with systems they use immediately."

MARK:   "I don't have time for a training day.
         We're slammed."

CALLER: "I hear you. Here's the thing though—
         that's exactly why this matters.
         Your agents are working 60 hours but
         losing 10 of those to admin.

         Train once, get 5-10 hours back
         per agent, every week.
         When's your slow season?"

MARK:   "January, usually."

CALLER: "Perfect. We book 4-6 weeks out anyway.
         Would a January date work to get this
         off your plate before spring hits?"

MARK:   "...Actually, that might make sense.
         What's this cost?"

CALLER: "$5,000 for the full day. For 8 agents,
         that's $625 per head. If they each
         get even 3 hours back per week,
         you've made the money back month one."

MARK:   "Alright, send me some info. I'll look
         at it this weekend."

CALLER: "Happy to. But quick question—if the
         curriculum looks good, are you open
         to booking a January date?"

MARK:   "Yeah, if it makes sense, sure."

CALLER: "Great. I'll send the curriculum and
         a couple January options. I'll call you
         Monday to lock it down. Work for you?"

MARK:   "Yeah, Monday afternoon is better."

CALLER: "Done. Talk Monday, Mark."
```

**Why It Worked:**
- Didn't fold when he said "busy" (gentle push)
- Reframed "busy" as exactly why he needs this
- Found his slow season (timing objection solve)
- Qualified his "send info" response
- Got commitment to a follow-up call
- Specific day/time for next step

## Example 3: Turning Skepticism into a Booking

**Context:** Education Director, Regional Association, VA
**AI Opening Hook:** "Serving 2,000+ members across the valley—that's a lot of agents counting on you for quality education."

```
CALLER: "Hi Linda, this is Mike with AI Acceleration.
         I'm calling because you serve 2,000 members
         across the valley—that's a lot of agents
         looking to you for quality education."

LINDA:  "That's right. What is this about?"

CALLER: "Quick question: Are your members asking
         about AI? How to use it, what tools to pick?"

LINDA:  "Oh god, yes. Every meeting, every event.
         Everyone wants to know about AI but
         honestly, I'm skeptical. I've seen
         so many tech fads come and go."

CALLER: "Totally fair. What makes you skeptical?"

LINDA:  "Agents try ChatGPT, get generic junk,
         and give up. Then they blame the tool
         instead of learning to use it right."

CALLER: "That's exactly the problem we solve.
         Most AI training is 'here's what AI can do.'
         We teach 'here's how YOU do it.'

         Hands-on. Agents build their systems live.
         They leave with prompts for listings,
         emails, social—ready to use.

         If it doesn't work, I want to know why."

LINDA:  "Hmm. We've done AI webinars before.
         Attendance was good but nobody
         changed their behavior."

CALLER: "Webinars have 5% implementation rates.
         Ours is 100%—because they're building
         in the room, not watching slides.

         Plus, we cover fraud detection.
         $500 million in wire fraud last year.
         That's not optional anymore."

LINDA:  "The fraud angle is interesting. Our
         members have been worried about that."

CALLER: "We spend 30 minutes on it. Red flags,
         verification protocols, voice deepfakes.
         Practical stuff.

         Here's the model: You host the training.
         We deliver. Lenders and title companies
         sponsor it—they want member access.
         They cover some or all of the cost.
         Your members get free or cheap training."

LINDA:  "Wait—sponsors cover the cost?"

CALLER: "Often, yes. They pay $1-2K each for
         face time with your members.
         2-3 sponsors and it's revenue-neutral for you."

LINDA:  "That's actually interesting. We have
         our annual conference in March..."

CALLER: "Perfect. We could do a breakout session
         or a standalone event the day before.
         Want me to send the curriculum and
         sponsor model? Then we can talk about
         how to fit it into March."

LINDA:  "Yes, send that. Can you call me next week?"

CALLER: "Absolutely. Tuesday or Thursday better?"

LINDA:  "Thursday, late morning."

CALLER: "Got it. I'll send everything today and
         call you Thursday at 11. Sound good?"

LINDA:  "Sounds good. Thanks, Mike."
```

**Why It Worked:**
- Didn't argue with skepticism (asked "what makes you skeptical?")
- Used her exact concern to position the solution
- Introduced fraud angle when opportunity arose
- Sponsor model was the turning point
- Connected to her existing event (March conference)
- Locked specific follow-up time

## Example 4: The "Already Using AI" Conversion

**Context:** Managing Broker, 22 agents, Lexington KY
**AI Opening Hook:** "Just saw your team won the Top Volume award—22 agents doing $180M is no joke."

```
CALLER: "Hi David, this is Amy with AI Acceleration.
         I saw your team won Top Volume—
         $180M with 22 agents is impressive."

DAVID:  "Thanks. What can I do for you?"

CALLER: "Quick question: How are your agents
         using AI right now? Any tools they love?"

DAVID:  "Actually, we're already pretty into it.
         Most of my team uses ChatGPT for
         listings and social media stuff."

CALLER: "That's great—you're ahead of most.
         How consistently are they using it?
         Like, every listing, or just sometimes?"

DAVID:  "Uh... probably more sometimes.
         A few agents use it all the time,
         most try it occasionally."

CALLER: "And when they use it, are they getting
         stuff they can use right away, or
         do they have to edit a lot?"

DAVID:  "There's usually editing involved.
         It doesn't sound like them, you know?"

CALLER: "Exactly. That's the gap between
         using AI and using it well.

         What we teach is context engineering—
         building a 'Voice Card' so AI outputs
         sound like your brand, your agents.

         Once they build it, no more editing.
         Paste it in, get usable output."

DAVID:  "Huh. We haven't done that."

CALLER: "Most haven't. That's the missing piece.
         One day, on-site, your agents build
         their personal systems. Prompts ready
         to go, no more generic garbage.

         For a team doing $180M, even a 5%
         productivity bump is huge."

DAVID:  "What does something like that cost?"

CALLER: "$5,000 for the full day. 22 agents,
         that's $225 per head. Less than
         a closing dinner."

DAVID:  "That's reasonable. When could you do it?"

CALLER: "I have January or early February.
         What works better for your team?"

DAVID:  "January's tight with holidays.
         First week of February?"

CALLER: "Let me check... Feb 5th or 6th?"

DAVID:  "6th could work. Let me talk to
         my office manager about logistics."

CALLER: "Perfect. Let me send you the curriculum.
         Can I call you Friday to confirm the 6th?"

DAVID:  "Yeah, Friday works."

CALLER: "Great. Talk then, David."
```

**Why It Worked:**
- Didn't accept "already using AI" at face value
- Asked probing questions (consistency, editing)
- Found the gap (outputs need editing)
- Positioned solution to that specific gap
- Tied ROI to their achievement ($180M volume)
- Got soft commitment before hanging up

## Common Patterns in Successful Calls

### 1. The Hook Gets Attention
Every good call starts with a personalized hook.
Generic openings get hung up on.

### 2. Permission Before Pitching
"Mind if I ask one question?" creates consent.
Pitching without permission creates resistance.

### 3. Let Them Talk First
The prospect reveals their real pain.
Your job is to listen, not lecture.

### 4. Connect Solution to Their Problem
Don't pitch features. Pitch their words back to them.
"You mentioned X... that's exactly what we solve."

### 5. Price is Confident, Not Apologetic
"$5,000 for the full day" (confident)
Not: "It's $5,000 but we can discuss..." (weak)

### 6. Always Get a Next Step
Never hang up without:
- A booked meeting, OR
- A specific follow-up date/time

## The Call Structure That Works

```
┌─────────────────────────────────────────┐
│           SUCCESSFUL CALL               │
├─────────────────────────────────────────┤
│ 0:00 - 0:15  Personalized Hook          │
│ 0:15 - 0:30  Permission Ask             │
│ 0:30 - 1:30  Discovery (THEY talk)      │
│ 1:30 - 2:00  Confirm Pain               │
│ 2:00 - 3:00  Value Proposition          │
│ 3:00 - 3:30  Handle Objection (if any)  │
│ 3:30 - 4:00  Close / Next Step          │
└─────────────────────────────────────────┘
```

Total time: 3-5 minutes for a booking.
