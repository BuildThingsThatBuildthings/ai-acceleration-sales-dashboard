## 2. VOICE FRAUD: Cloning & Audio Deepfakes

### **RED FLAGS**

#### **Call Quality Indicators**
â–¡ **Slightly robotic** or flat intonation
â–¡ **Unusual pauses** or timing
â–¡ **Background noise cuts out** unnaturally
â–¡ **Audio quality changes** mid-conversation
â–¡ **Caller ID spoofing** (shows legitimate number but sounds off)

#### **Behavioral Red Flags**
â–¡ **Urgent financial requests** ("Wire money now")
â–¡ **Emotional manipulation** ("I'm in trouble, need cash")
â–¡ **Refuses video call** or in-person meeting
â–¡ **Avoids personal details** only you and real person would know
â–¡ **Pressure to act immediately** without verification

---

### **VERIFICATION PROTOCOL: Voice Communications**

**STEP 1:** âœ… **Establish pre-arranged code word** with frequent contacts
- Family members
- Title officers
- Attorneys
- Lenders
- Clients in active transactions

**STEP 2:** âœ… **Ask verification questions** only real person would know
- "What was the first property I showed you?"
- "What's your dog's name?"
- "What restaurant did we meet at last month?"

**STEP 3:** âœ… **Hang up and call back** using known number
- DO NOT use call-back number provided by caller
- Look up number independently

**STEP 4:** âœ… **Request video call** if possible
- Deepfake video exists but is harder to fake in real-time
- Ask them to do specific actions (wave, hold up object)

**STEP 5:** âœ… **Trust your gut**
- If something feels "off," it probably is
- Better to over-verify than lose money

â–¡ **Seasonal mismatches**: Summer greenery but listing date is winter

### **VERIFICATION PROTOCOL: Images**

**STEP 1:** âœ… **Reverse image search**

- TinEye.com: Specialized reverse search
- Check if photo appears on multiple listings

**STEP 2:** âœ… **Cross-reference with MLS**
- Verify photos match MLS listing
- Check upload dates match listing timeline

**STEP 3:** âœ… **Request original, unedited photos** from seller/photographer

**STEP 4:** âœ… **Schedule in-person showing**

**STEP 5:** âœ… **Disclose enhancements**

## Report Fraud Immediately

**If you suspect fraud:**

1. **Stop**: Do not proceed with wire transfer or transaction
2. **Verify**: Call known contact to confirm legitimacy
3. **Report**:
   - FBI IC3: www.ic3.gov
   - FTC: reportfraud.ftc.gov
   - Local police
   - Your title company
   - Your brokerage compliance officer

**Time matters**: Many banks can reverse wires if caught within hours
