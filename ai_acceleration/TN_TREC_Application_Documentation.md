# Tennessee Real Estate Commission
## Continuing Education Course Application - Supporting Documentation

**Course Title:** AI Acceleration: AI Tools for Real Estate Professionals
**Provider:** Build Things that Build Things
**Instructor:** Ryan Wanner, Founder & CEO
**Presented to:** Williamson County Association of REALTORS® (WCAR)
**Duration:** 7 hours (9:00 AM - 4:00 PM)
**Format:** 70% hands-on practice, 30% instruction

---

## 1. PURPOSE OF COURSE

This course provides a practical ramp-up for real estate professionals on how to think about AI, where it adds value, and how to communicate with it for useful, safe outputs. Participants learn what AI is and isn't, prompting fundamentals, context engineering, identifying jobs AI can replace or augment, choosing tools by job-to-be-done, and basic AI fraud awareness.

---

## 2. HOW THIS COURSE IMPROVES PROFESSIONALISM

Agents gain clear understanding of what AI is, where it fits in real estate workflows, and how to use it for useful, safe outputs. They learn to maintain brand voice, identify appropriate job-to-be-done applications, protect clients from AI-enabled fraud, and select tools that integrate with professional workflows. This practical knowledge helps agents adopt AI technology competently and responsibly.

---

## 3. OTHER PROFESSIONS/COMMISSIONS FOR CONTINUING EDUCATION CREDITS

None. This course is designed exclusively for licensed real estate professionals and the provider is seeking continuing education credit from the Tennessee Real Estate Commission only.

---

## 4. TENNESSEE REAL ESTATE COMMISSION TCA OR RULE - RULE 1260-5-.03

**Rule 1260-5-.03 - Continuing Education Requirements**

**Course Length:** 7 classroom hours with 330 minutes of instruction, meeting minimum hour requirements.

**Emerging Issues:** Addresses AI technology adoption, fraud prevention (wire fraud, voice cloning, deepfakes, document forgery), Fair Housing compliance for AI-generated content, and data privacy protocols. Rule 1260-5-.03 requires education on "current laws, rules, and emerging issues affecting real estate practice."

**Real Estate Relevance:** All content uses real estate scenarios—property descriptions, client emails, market summaries, transaction tasks, fraud detection for wire transfers. Teaches practical technology skills agents use daily.

**Assessment:** Includes 6 hands-on exercises with documented learning objectives, 10-point capstone project rubric, and 100% attendance tracking.

**Category:** Technology in Real Estate (primary); Risk Management (secondary).
