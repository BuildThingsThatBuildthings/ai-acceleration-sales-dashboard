## Technical Requirements

### Instructor Equipment

**Must-Have:**
- Laptop (Mac or PC, recent model for speed)
  - AI-capable (runs ChatGPT/Claude smoothly)
  - HDMI and USB-C ports (or adapters)
  - Fully charged + power cable
- Presentation remote/clicker (backup: use laptop spacebar)
- Backup USB drive with all slides and demo files
- Phone (for timing, backup hotspot if WiFi fails)
- Water bottle (stay hydrated for 7-hour day)

**Highly Recommended:**
- Second monitor or tablet (for speaker notes while presenting)
- Portable WiFi hotspot (backup if venue WiFi crashes)
- Extension cord/power strip (for laptop + phone charging)
- HDMI + USB-C + VGA adapters (cover all projector types)
- Bluetooth speaker (if room audio is poor for fraud demo)
- Laser pointer (for highlighting slide details)

### Venue Requirements

**Room Setup:**
- Capacity: 25-30 participants
- Layout: Tables (not theater-style) to accommodate laptops
- Projector + screen (or large TV)
  - Minimum 100" diagonal screen for room size
  - 1080p resolution minimum
- Sound system:
  - Microphone if room > 30 people
  - Audio input for laptop (for fraud demo voicemail)
- Whiteboard or flip chart:
  - Minimum 4'x6' size
  - Markers and eraser
- Power outlets:
  - 1 outlet per 2 participants minimum
  - Extension cords if needed

**WiFi Requirements:**
- Bandwidth: Support 25-30 simultaneous ChatGPT/Claude connections
- Speed: Minimum 100 Mbps down / 20 Mbps up
- Guest network available (no complicated login/VPN)
- Password simple enough to write on whiteboard
- Backup: Instructor has mobile hotspot ready

**Logistics:**
- Break area with coffee/snacks
- Lunch catering (or nearby food options)
- Restrooms accessible
- Parking instructions clear
- Temperature control (A/C for 30 people with laptops)

### Participant Requirements

**Recommended (Not Required):**
- Laptop or tablet with internet browser
  - Any device that can access chat.openai.com or claude.ai
  - Tablet keyboards helpful for typing prompts
  - Phones work but are less ideal for exercises

**What Participants DO NOT Need:**
- Paid AI subscriptions (free ChatGPT/Claude sufficient)
- Software installations (everything browser-based)
- Technical expertise (workshop starts from zero)

**What to Bring:**
- Real content to work on (property listings, client emails, market data)
- Notebook and pen (for notes)
- Charger for laptop/device

**Type**: Strict timing - sets tone for day

#### SLIDE 1: Title Slide (2 min)

**SAY:**
> "Good morning! I'm Ryan. Welcome to AI Acceleration. Before we dive in, I want to set expectations. This is NOT a typical training. Here's what won't happen today: death by PowerPoint, jargon overload, tools you'll never use, theory without practice. Here's what WILL happen: you'll create real AI outputs for your actual business in the first hour. You'll leave with working systems you can use Monday morning. Everything we do today passes one test: does it save you time or make you money? If not, we skip it."

**DO:**
- Stand center stage, make eye contact with different sections of room
- Speak with energy and conviction (not salesy, confident)
- Pause after "Here's what won't happen" for emphasis

**Q&A PROMPT:**
> "Show of hands: Who's tried ChatGPT or another AI tool? Who uses it weekly? Who's skeptical this will actually work for real estate?"

**DO:**
- Count hands for each question (calibrates room experience level)
- Acknowledge skeptics with respect: "Good - healthy skepticism keeps us honest"
- Note experience distribution mentally (will inform pacing)

#### SLIDE 1: Mental Models - Your AI Cheat Codes (3 min)

**SAY:**
> "Story time. Two agents both try AI for listing descriptions. Agent A gives up in 10 minutes saying 'it doesn't work.' Agent B generates 5 great listings in 20 minutes. Same tool. ## Common Questions & Answers

### Pre-Workshop Questions

**Q: "Do I need to pay for ChatGPT Plus or Claude Pro?"**
A: "No. Free versions work fine for this workshop. I'll show you when paid versions help, but they're not required."

**Q: "I'm not technical. Will I be able to do this?"**
A: "Absolutely. We start from zero. If you can use Google, you can use AI. It's browser-based, no installations."

**Q: "What if I don't bring a laptop?"**
A: "You can pair with someone, use your phone, or just watch and take notes. You'll still learn the concepts and can practice later."

### During Workshop Questions

**Q: "Can AI pull current MLS data?"**
A: "No. AI doesn't have access to live databases. You provide the facts, AI writes the words. Think of it as a copywriter, not a researcher."

**Q: "Is this legal? Can I use AI-written content commercially?"**
A: "Yes. You own the outputs. But you're responsible for accuracy and compliance, which is why we verify everything."

**Q: "What about Fair Housing compliance?"**
A: "AI doesn't know Fair Housing law. That's why we use the R in CLEAR - Restrictions. You tell AI what NOT to say."

**Q: "Which AI tool should I use - ChatGPT, Claude, or something else?"**
A: "Start with free ChatGPT or Claude. They're 90% similar. We'll cover tool selection in Module 9."

**Q: "My output is garbage. What am I doing wrong?"**
A: "Usually it's missing facts or context. Walk me through your prompt - let's apply CLEAR."

**Q: "How do I know if AI made something up?"**
A: "Trust but verify. Check every fact against your source. If AI says '4 bedrooms' and MLS says '3,' catch it."

**Q: "Can I upload client data to ChatGPT?"**
A: "Free ChatGPT stores data. For client PII, either anonymize it or use paid ChatGPT Plus with data protection. We'll cover this in fraud module."

**Q: "This seems like cheating."**
A: "Is using spell-check cheating? Is using a calculator? AI is a tool. You're still doing the thinking, verifying, and client relationship."

## Troubleshooting Guide

### Technology Issues

**Issue**: WiFi down or slow
**Solution**:
- Use mobile hotspot (have ready)
- Show pre-saved screenshots of demos
- Have participants use phone data temporarily
- Worst case: teach concepts without live demos, participants practice at home

**Issue**: AI tool (ChatGPT/Claude) not loading
**Solution**:
- Switch to alternate tool (ChatGPT ↔ Claude)
- Use mobile version on phone, project via screen mirror
- Show pre-generated outputs from slides
- Continue teaching, participants can practice later

**Issue**: Projector fails
**Solution**:
- Use large TV if available
- Screen share to participants' devices if small group
- Print backup slides (one-pager summaries)
- Whiteboard everything (old school but effective)

### Participant Challenges

**Issue**: Participant completely lost/overwhelmed
**Solution**:
- Pair them with advanced participant
- Spend 1-on-1 time during practice sessions
- Lower expectations: "Just watch and take notes, practice later"
- Check in at breaks: "What's confusing? Let's clarify"

**Issue**: Participant finishes exercises way too fast
**Solution**:
- Give them advanced challenges: "Try creating 3 variations"
- Ask them to help struggling neighbor
- Have them experiment with edge cases
- Bonus: "Create templates for your team"

**Issue**: Participant's AI output is inappropriate/offensive
**Solution**:
- Quietly review their prompt (likely missing Restrictions)
- Teach: "This is why R in CLEAR matters - tell AI what NOT to say"
- Have them add exclusions and regenerate
- Don't shame - frame as learning moment

### Content Issues

**Issue**: Fraud demos are too scary/alarming
**Solution**:
- Balance with solutions: "This is preventable with verification"
- Emphasize you're more informed than 95% of agents now
- Share success stories of fraud caught early

**Issue**: Participants want to automate weak-fit tasks
**Solution**:
- Redirect: "Let's start with strong fit, build skills, then tackle harder jobs"
- Explain risk: "If AI handles negotiations, who's liable when it fails?"
- Show pathway: "Master drafts first, then we can discuss judgment tasks"

## Energy Management

### Morning (9:00-12:00) - Peak Energy
**Strategy**: Complex concepts, new learning, foundation building
**Instructor Energy**: High, enthusiastic, engaging
**Techniques**:
- Stand, move around room
- Use big gestures
- Call on participants frequently
- Vary voice tone and pace
- Keep slides visually dynamic

### Post-Lunch (1:00-2:00) - Lower Energy
**Strategy**: Interactive exercises, hands-on work, movement
**Instructor Energy**: Maintain high to counteract participant dip
**Techniques**:
- Get participants moving (whiteboard voting, pair-shares)
- Use timer challenges (gamify exercises)
- Tell stories (narrative engages sleepy brains)
- Circulate during practice (physical presence energizes)
- Play upbeat music during exercise work time (optional)

### Afternoon (2:10-4:00) - Recovery Energy
**Strategy**: Application, synthesis, capstone (self-directed work)
**Instructor Energy**: Supportive, coaching mode
**Techniques**:
- More participant-led (they present, you facilitate)
- Break into smaller practice chunks (10 min work, 2 min debrief)
- Use peer feedback (they energize each other)
- Celebrate wins loudly (positive reinforcement)
- Countdown to finish (creates urgency and focus)

### Break Strategy
**10-Minute Breaks**:
- Strictly enforce 10 minutes (use timer)
- Encourage movement (stand, stretch, walk)
- Provide caffeine/snacks if possible

**Lunch Break**:
- Offer optional office hours (1-on-1 help for stuck participants)
- Encourage them to step away from screens
- Set clear restart time: "Back at 1:00 PM sharp"
